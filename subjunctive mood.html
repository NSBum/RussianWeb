Subjunctive mood


(See the discussion on optative mood for a description of the linguistic roll that moods play in semantics.)


The subjunctive mood is a way of expressing a hypothetical condition. It sounds a bit odd or stiff in conversation in English but it important in many other languages. In English the subjunctive mood could be rendered as "I will ensure that he returns the money to you tomorrow."  (Note the proper use of the form returns in the English.)
