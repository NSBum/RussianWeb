Gender in Russian nouns


As opposed to some other languages that assign gender to nouns, it is much easier to discern in Russian. First, the rules about gender, then the exceptions and edge cases.


  1. Nouns that end in -а or -я are feminine (with some exceptions, especially where the natural gender is easily discernible as with папа or дядя)
  2. Most nouns that end in a consonant are masculine. (Some foreign names of women and some words loaned from English are exceptions, e.g. мадам)
  3. Most nouns ending in -о or -e are neuter.


Nouns ending in the soft sign -ь


Nouns that end in the soft sign may be either masculine or feminine. This is really the only obscure area of discerning gender in Russian nouns.


To determine if a noun ending in -ь is masculine:


  1. If it refers to a male person, e.g. парень, учитель, etc.
  2. The months of the year, e.g. июнь, январь, etc.
  3. If the ending is -тель, e.g. двигатель (engine)


To determine if a noun ending in -ь is feminine:


  1. If it refers to a female person, e.g. дочь, мать, etc.
  2. If the preceding consonant is ч, ж, ш, щ.
  3. If the ending is -ость, -есть.
