Accusative case


The accusative case is used for the direct objects of verbs.


Nouns in Russian are divided into animate and inanimate groups; and nouns are treated differently in the accusative case depending on which group they are in.

Inanimate nouns do not change their ending from the nominative to accusative cases.  So, ночь is still ночь in the accusative case. That's an inanimate feminine noun. Other feminine nouns that end in -а оr -я change to -у or -ю respectively. That's also true of masculine nouns with the  -а оr -я endings. So девочка would be девочку in the accusative.


When used with a preposition, it indicates a dynamic location.
