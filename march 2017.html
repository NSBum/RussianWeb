2017-03-15


Beginning to reconsider my approach of simply working down the 10,000 word list. The lack of textual context and the sheer volume of that project is beginning to cause my enthusiasm to come to a grinding halt. Not ready to abandon the idea yet, but maybe slow down the rate at which I'm adding new material so that I can give more time to processing the words that are coming in. The risk, of course, in this sort of frequency-ordered memorization, is that it just becomes rote and that it's not really possible to incorporate it all in a functional way.  


Read this text from the UCLA Intermediate to Advanced text and listened to the accompanying audio.


Learned and interesting phrase "без особого энтузиазма" - without (any) particular enthusiasm. особого is the genitive singular of особый - special, particular, peculiar.

This sentence "Но когда она увидела свою учительницу, то забыла о том, что не хотела идти в школу." is a little confusing in its construction. The meaning is reasonably clear: "But when she saw her teacher, she forgot the she didn't want to go to school." Here, то confused me a bit. I think it roughly means "then", as in a logical conclusion of the first clause. In that role (it has other potential semantic roles), it's a sort of conjunctive particle. The construction that follows then makes sense; it's just a то, что construction. See page 148-149 in the Comprehensive Russian Grammar book (Wade). It takes a slightly different, but important form because you forget about something (о том).


This sentence "Она была молодой..." confused me slightly at first. Молодой looks like the masculine nominative singular. But actually, it's the instrumental case. She was young (condition, status → instrumental)
