This chapter deals with major subject of Russian semantics - the verbs of motion. This subject is one of the hards for non-native speakers to grasp because it's a concept that most languages, English, included do not have. The key distinction that allows the speaker to choose the right motion verb is whether a given motion involves multiple directions or a single direction.

20.3 Where aspect meets the verbs of motion


This can be tricky to understand.


The 14 verb pairs in the table at the beginning of the chapter are all imperfective, but all also have a perfective partner made by prefixing по-. This means that there are 4 forms for each verbal concept. The effect of the prefix по- with multidirectional verbs means "a little bit", "for a little while", etc. Basically a diminutive. For unidirectional verbs, it means either "to start" or "to make a complete trip in one direction."


Supplementary books on verbs of motion


Verbs of Motion in Russian, L. Murayova - I have a very old edition of this classic text and it is well used.  The old school illustrations actually do aid in understanding the concepts. The book is recommended; but make sure you have enough vocabulary at your disposal to make it worthwhile.
