Spaced repetition learning


Traditional flashcard memorization are inefficient because they make no distinction between information that is already known and information that needs to be reviewed. Spaced repetition learning attempts to solve the efficiency problem by tracking how well a given piece of information is, then scheduling its repetition accordingly. Of course, without repetition, memory retention rates are very low, following a predicatable forgetting curve. The opposite problem occurs with indiscriminately frequent memorization. There, the retention rate is very high, but at the cost of wasting time reviewing material that is already well-known.


 


By reviewing the material to be learned at intervals that are determined by past performance, you gain efficiency in the learning process.
