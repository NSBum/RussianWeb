Aspect of Russian verbs


The concept of aspect is one of the most unique features of the Russian verbal system. The other, of course, is its treatment of the verbs of motion.


Aspect, in its most elementary form, is a way of distinguishing activities that were completed or have a specific goal from those that do not. The vast majority of Russian verbs have two aspects: imperfective and perfective. Imperfective verbs describe actions that have not been completed or are performed habitually. Perfective verbs, on the other hand are used, in part, to describe actions that were completed. Like all things in Russian grammar, it's not quite that simple, though.
