choice of multidirectional or unidirectional verbs

Rules governing the choice of multidirectional or unidirectional verbs in verbs of motion


  1. If the motion involves multiple directions, use the multidirectional form.
  2. If there is no motion, use the multidirectional form.
  3. If the number of directions isn't relevant, use the multidirectional form.


If the motion involves multiple directions, use the multidirectional form.


This is the most obvious of the rules.


Я хожу в школу каждый день.  I go to school every day.

Going to school every day requires multiple round-trips, so we use the multidirectional form ходить.


Здесь идёт дождь каждый день. It rains every day here.

Although this is describing a habitual act (it rains every day), we use the unidirectional form идти because rain only goes in one direction (down).


Мы часто ездим в Торонто.  We often go (by transport) to Toronto.

Again, the sentence describes multiple round-trips to Toronto.


Луна бегала во дворе. Luna ran around in the yard.

Since Luna is a dog who runs in many directions, we use бегатъ the multidirectional form.


Дети идут домой. The children are going home.

The sentence is describing a single direction so we choose the unidirectional form идти.


If there is no motion, use the multidirectional form.


Он никогда не летает. He never flies.

There's no motion here. That is, the sentence describes the absence of an indeterminate number of potential trips. So we chose the multidirectional form.


If the number of directions isn't relevant, use the multidirectional form.


Вы летали когда-нибудь на самолёте? Have you ever flown in an airplane?

Here we're asking about an indeterminate number of potential trips in an airplane, presumably involving multiple directions. 


Я люблю плавать. I like to swim.

The act of swimming means moving in many directions, but the number isn't relevant since I'm talking about the concept of swimming.
