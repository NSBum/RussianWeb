Optative mood


In grammar, moods are a feature of verbs and their surrounding words and structures that convey modality or the subject's or speaker's attitude toward their expression. It is different from tense and time because they both have to do with time.


The optative mood, which overlaps somewhat with subjunctive mood, expresses a wish or desire. (Some languages have a formal distinct optative mood. Russian does not.)
